BIR Base (purée + onion water)

The two-part foundation of every house curry. One Ninja pressure-cook makes enough for two curries.

Active15 min
Pressure12-15 min
Yields2 curries
Used byall BIR curries

Ingredients

Method

  1. Everything into the Ninja, water just to cover. Pressure cook 12-15 minutes.
  2. Fish out the whole spices and discard.
  3. Blend the solids (onion + carrot) to a thick, smooth purée: ~900 g, i.e. 450 g per curry.
  4. Keep the cooking liquid as the onion water: ~400 ml per curry; top up with hot kettle water if short.
The two jobs: the purée is the bit you fry hard to deep amber (the flavour); the onion water is the liquid, added hot after the fry. Mild creamy curries (korma, pasanda) are the exception: fry only to pale golden. Full doctrine in The BIR Curry System. Both parts freeze well.
