Homemade Chilli Powder

The house Mexican blend: three toasted dried chiles ground with warm spices. Fruity, smoky, miles beyond any jar.

Makes12 tbsp (≈3 batches of chili)
Keeps6 months airtight
Used byVegan Chili · Fajitas
SourceRainbow Plant Life

Ingredients

  • Dried chiles
  • Spices

Method

  1. Stem the chiles, tear open, scrape out seeds and membranes (this is what prevents bitterness). Wash hands after.
  2. Toast the anchos and guajillos in a dry, heavy pan over medium, single layer: ~1 minute, flip, 30-60 seconds. Fragrant, never scorched.
  3. Toast the árbols separately, 30-45 seconds.
  4. Toast the cumin and coriander seeds 45-60 seconds, stirring. Cool everything completely.
  5. Grind the chiles and toasted seeds to a fine powder, then blend in the pre-ground spices.
  6. Airtight jar, cool and dark, up to 6 months.
House doctrine: this is the chilli powder for all Mexican/Tex-Mex cooking (Vegan Chili, Fajitas). It already carries cumin, oregano, garlic and onion, so recipes using it should halve those additions. It is NOT for Indian dishes; that's Kashmiri's job, via the BIR Mix Powder.
