Roast Chicken Dinner

The family Sunday roast: herb-butter chicken, proper crispy potatoes, roast veg and pan gravy.

Prep15 min
Cook90 min
Serves4-6
SourceFamily recipe

Ingredients

  • Roast chicken
  • Roast potatoes
  • Vegetables (optional)
  • Gravy

Method

Chicken

  1. Dry-brine 24 hours ahead: pat the chicken dry and salt all over with ~¾ tsp flaky salt per 500 g. Sit it uncovered on a rack in the fridge overnight (this seasons through and crisps the skin).
  2. Spatchcock: with sturdy scissors, cut down both sides of the backbone and remove it. Turn the bird breast-up and press firmly to flatten. Take it out of the fridge 30 minutes ahead.
  3. Preheat the oven to 220°C fan. Mix the softened butter with minced garlic, chopped thyme and rosemary; season with pepper (the salt is already on the bird).
  4. Loosen the breast and thigh skin and spread half the herb butter underneath; rub the rest over the outside.
  5. Halve the lemon, squeeze over and tuck the spent halves and remaining herb sprigs under the bird.
  6. Lay the spatchcocked chicken flat in a roasting tin, drizzle with oil. Roast at 220°C fan for ~45-50 minutes.
  7. Pull it at 66°C in the thickest part of the breast (74°C in the thigh); the breast will carry over to a safe finish without overcooking. Rest 15-20 minutes before carving.

Potatoes

  1. Peel, cut into large chunks (~5 cm), parboil in salted water 10 minutes, drain well and rough up the edges in the colander.
  2. Heat the oil in a roasting tin in the oven until very hot (~5 minutes).
  3. Add the potatoes to the hot oil, turn to coat, season with salt and rosemary. Roast 50-60 minutes, turning halfway.

Vegetables

  1. Peel and chunk the carrots and parsnips, toss in oil, salt and pepper, and roast alongside the chicken for the final 45 minutes.

Gravy

  1. With the chicken resting, pour off excess fat from the tin, keeping ~2 tbsp and all the sticky bits.
  2. Tin on the hob, medium heat: add the wine (if using) and scrape up the fond. Add stock and simmer.
  3. Stir in the cornflour paste; simmer 5-10 minutes to thicken. Season; strain if you want it smooth.
Notes: the dry brine and spatchcock are the standard method here: faster, more even cooking and crisper skin. A digital probe is worth it, pull at 66°C breast / 74°C thigh. Save the backbone and carcass for stock. Leftovers: sandwiches, salads, chicken pie.
