Fish and Chips

Beer-battered fish with air-fried chips: crisp shell, juicy fish, no deep-fat fryer required for the chips.

Prep25 min
Cook45 min
Serves4
SourceEthan Chlebowski

Ingredients

  • Fish & batter
  • Chips

Method

  1. Chips: peel and cut the potatoes into chip-sized chunks. Toss with salt and a spray of olive oil. Air-fry at 200°C for 20-25 minutes, shaking occasionally, until golden and crisp.
  2. Fish: cut into 4 equal portions. Mix 50 g flour with a teaspoon of salt for the coating; set aside.
  3. Batter: combine 100 g flour, 50 g cornflour, baking powder, a teaspoon of salt, pepper and the vinegar. Gradually whisk in the beer to a consistency between crepe and pancake batter. Don't overmix.
  4. Heat the oil to 5 cm depth in a wok or deep pan, to 176°C (350°F).
  5. Coat each fish piece in flour, shake off the excess, dip in batter, let the excess drip, then lower gently into the oil. Fry 2 at a time, 4-5 minutes, turning occasionally, until golden and the fish hits 60°C inside.
  6. Drain on a wire rack, dab with kitchen paper, sprinkle with salt.
  7. Serve the fish with the air-fried chips.
