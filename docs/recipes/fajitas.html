Fajitas

Chicken, peppers and onions seared hard and piled into warm tortillas at the table. The definitive house version, built on the Homemade Chilli Powder.

Prep~15 min
Cook~15 min
Serves4
SourceHouse (ATK + RecipeTin)

Ingredients

  • Spice mix
  • To cook
  • To serve

Method

  1. Mix the spice blend. Toss the sliced chicken with the spices, garlic, oil, lime and orange juice. If you have time, leave 30 to 60 minutes; if not, straight on.
  2. Heat a large heavy pan with a little light olive oil until just smoking. Sear the chicken in a single, uncrowded layer, undisturbed ~4 minutes, then flip for 2 to 4 minutes more, to just cooked through (71°C). Lift out and rest.
  3. In the same hot pan, sear the peppers and onions hard in one layer until charred at the edges but still with bite. (Char them in the pizza oven if it's already lit.)
  4. Slice the rested chicken across the grain; return it to the pan with the veg to combine.
  5. Squeeze fresh lime over off the heat. Pile into warm tortillas with the toppings.
Why so few added spices: the Homemade Chilli Powder already carries cumin, oregano, garlic and onion, so this leans on it and adds only what lifts a fajita past the chili profile: a little extra cumin and smoked paprika, salt, pepper, a touch of sugar. No cornflour (it suits a saucy or sheet-pan version, but works against a hard-seared fajita). Lime goes in the marinade and again at the table.

Blend and method reconciled from America's Test Kitchen, RecipeTin Eats and Serious Eats.
