Dakgalbi

Korean spicy chicken stir-fry with rice cakes and sweet potato, cooked in one big pan everyone eats from. The hottest of the Korean trials, fajitas-level table theatre.

Prep25 min
Cook15 min
Serves4
SourceBeyond Kimchee
StatusTrial

Ingredients

  • The pan
  • Sauce
  • Optional finale

Method

  1. Mix the sauce; reserve 1 tbsp if you're doing the fried-rice finale. Marinate the diced chicken in the rest for 30 minutes, ideally a few hours. (Set aside a plain portion for the kids before it goes in.)
  2. Soak the rice cakes 10 minutes; drain.
  3. Big pan or cast iron, medium-high: add the oil, then the marinated chicken with its sauce, the vegetables, rice cakes and half the perilla.
  4. Stir-fry 6-7 minutes until the chicken is cooked and sweet potato tender, splashing in water if it sticks.
  5. Finish with the remaining perilla and sesame seeds. Pan goes to the table.
  6. Finale: when the pan is nearly empty, fry the rice in the leftovers with the reserved sauce until it crisps, 2 minutes.
Trial notes: the spiciest of the six trials; for the kids, set aside marinated-but-unsauced chicken and veg before the gochujang goes in and cook their corner separately, or save this one for an adults' night. The crispy fried-rice finale is the bit everyone remembers.
