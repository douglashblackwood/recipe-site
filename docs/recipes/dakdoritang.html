Dakdoritang (Spicy Chicken Stew)

Korean braised chicken with potatoes in a gochujang-gochugaru sauce. Has an official slow-cooker variant, so the Ninja was made for it.

Cook30 min hob, or 4-6 h slow cook
Serves4
SourceKorean Bapsang
StatusTrial

Ingredients

  • Chicken & veg
  • Sauce

Method

  1. Parboil the chicken pieces ~1 minute in boiling water; drain and rinse (especially for the hob, bone-in version, for a cleaner sauce).
  2. Hob: 350 ml water, chicken and sauce in a pot; boil covered 15 minutes.
  3. Stir in the vegetables; cover and cook 10 minutes.
  4. Uncover and cook ~5 minutes more until tender and the sauce thickens.
  5. Finish with spring onions, sesame oil and seeds. Rice alongside.
  6. Ninja slow-cook variant: everything except the finishes into the pot, stir; high 4 hours or low 6. Finish the same way. (Pressure mode: ~12 minutes, natural release, then reduce the sauce on sear.)
Trial notes: heat lives in the gochugaru, so scale it down for a family pot and let adults add chilli at the table; the gochujang base stays mild-warm and sweet. The set-and-forget variant is the answer to clinical-day dinners.
Mild family default: a gochujang-led ratio (more gochujang, less gochugaru) is the milder default for the whole family.

Source: Korean Bapsang · pressure timing is the house adaptation
