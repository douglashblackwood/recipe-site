Pizza

House dough, upgraded: less yeast, a 24-48 hour cold ferment, and cooked in the wood-fired pizza oven in 90 seconds. Makes 4.

Prep20 min
Ferment24-48 h (fridge)
Cook60-90 s each
Makes4 pizzas
SourceHouse recipe

Ingredients

  • Dough
  • Topping

Method

  1. Autolyse: mix the flour and 225 ml warm water until just combined. Rest 20-30 minutes.
  2. Activate the yeast: 3 g yeast in 50 ml plain warm water (27-29°C). Leave 5 minutes until frothy.
  3. Add the salt to the autolysed mixture, then the yeast mixture and optional oil. Mix on speed 2 until combined.
  4. Knead on speed 4 for 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic. Rest, covered, 20 minutes.
  5. Divide into 4 balls (180-200 g) and place in a lightly oiled, covered container.
  6. Cold ferment in the fridge, 24-48 hours (48 is better). This is where the flavour and browning come from.
  7. On the day, take the dough out 2 hours before cooking.
  8. Fire the pizza oven to full heat (400°C+ stone). Stretch, top, launch: 60-90 seconds, quarter-turning for even char. No par-bake.
  9. Top lightly: ~75 g sauce, ~80 g torn mozzarella per pizza. The oven is too fast for heavy toppings.
  10. Domestic oven fallback: max temperature, stone preheated 45 minutes; par-bake 3-5 minutes, top, then 7-10 minutes.
  11. Rest 2 minutes; finish with extra virgin olive oil and basil.
Upgraded 11/06/2026: yeast cut from 7 g to 3 g and the same-day prove replaced with a cold ferment; the bake moved to the pizza oven (the old domestic method is kept as the fallback). In a pinch, a same-day 4-5 hour prove still works with 5 g yeast.
