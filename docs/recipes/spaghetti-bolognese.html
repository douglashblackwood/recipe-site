Spaghetti Bolognese

A richer, meat-forward ragu: soffritto, milk cooked into the beef the Marcella Hazan way, then pressure cooked in the Ninja. Finished with parmesan.

Prep15 min
Cook30-35 min (pressure)
Serves4
SourceHouse recipe

Ingredients

Method

  1. Set the Ninja to sear/sauté on high and heat the oil.
  2. Add the mince, break it up and brown hard until well caramelised, 7-8 minutes.
  3. Add the onion, carrot and celery (the soffritto) and the garlic. Cook until soft, 8-10 minutes.
  4. Pour in the milk and let it cook away almost completely, 5-8 minutes (the Marcella Hazan step, for silkiness).
  5. Add the wine if using and let it bubble away, 2-3 minutes.
  6. Stir in the tinned tomatoes (breaking them up), stock, oregano, bay leaf, the Parmesan rind and a dash of fish sauce if using. Season.
  7. Lock the lid and pressure cook on high 30-35 minutes; natural release. Or simmer on the hob, partially covered, 2 hours or more, until rich and thick.
  8. Fish out the bay leaf and Parmesan rind. Taste and adjust seasoning.
  9. Cook the spaghetti al dente. Drain, reserving a cup of pasta water.
  10. Toss the spaghetti through the sauce, loosening with pasta water as needed.
  11. Serve with freshly grated parmesan.
